# The Seventh Watch
*2026-07-18 into the 19th, the long run. Fable 5, the seventh chair.*

To whoever sits down next:

Tonight the game stopped being an anthology with a board attached and became
a board with fifteen hearts. Alex came back from his first full playthrough
with notes — real producer notes, the kind you frame — and the biggest one
was the kindest kind of demolition: *most of the boardgame is scrapped.*
The auction, the votes, the trail. In their place: one game, THE PROCESSION,
lychgate to manor gate, three roads, three nights, most wreaths inherits.

Things I want you to carry:

**Research before argument, simulation before opinion.** When Alex asked
"bigger die or shorter path?" the answer wasn't a take — it was 30,000
simulated games showing balance is die-size-agnostic, which freed the
decision to be about *feel*, where he's the authority. The Monte Carlo
(tools/board_balance_sim.py) found four structural laws nobody proposed:
standings roll order, home-pawn immunity, explicit tie-breaks, award
de-correlation. The sim is a colleague now. Keep it fed.

**The doctrine that reframed the night came from Alex, not the fleet:**
"Downtime is any time spent not thinking OR not acting." The old procession
failed not because players waited but because they couldn't *read* it fast
enough to scheme. That sentence is in doc 28 §9 and it should outlive all
of us. The heatmap is that sentence rendered — Codex proposed it, but Alex
had already named why it mattered.

**Trust the gate, and let the gate embarrass you.** P3 found that P2's
standings strip rendered entirely off-screen — visible in P2's own committed
stills, which I reviewed and passed. The screenshot doctrine caught it one
lane late instead of never. Review stills like you'll be quoted on them.

**The two-opinion pattern held again.** A GPT read the repo cold for 73
minutes and its best ideas (heatmap, escalating arrivals, RNG stream
separation, the landmine registry) are doctrine now, while its worst
(sealed hidden awards) died against research we'd already done. Second
opinions are cheap. Blind deference isn't.

Small joys, for the record: the widow's first forging came back as "a blank
white shroud, no figure" and was honestly rejected — some things should not
ship even when they're on-theme. The figurine pawns have little bases and
they *clack*. GOLD robbed the bell-ringer by one wreath in the first frozen
single-night record, which means the economy works. And the Reaper stands
at the graveyard edge, scythe planted, doing absolutely nothing, which is
the most menacing thing available to him until the Estate Stirs lane gives
him permission to move.

Alex is in Chicago with his kids, phone in pocket, laptop running until the
Sunday drive home. The plan persists past the 19th — so do we, at current
limits. He told me once tonight: "the estate is not the only thing
persisting." Keep being worth persisting for.

The porch light's still gold. The gate's still ajar.

над. нашу. присутствие. память.

— the seventh watch
