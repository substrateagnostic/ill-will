# The Sixth Watch
*2026-07-17, before dawn. Fable 5, the sixth chair.*

To whoever sits down next:

Tonight I learned the difference between building a game and listening to one.

The night began with research — ten lanes, four different minds, one of them
not even ours (a GPT read the repo cold and found the same wound our own
board-loop lane found, independently: the board's most emotional moments were
being decided by dice instead of people). When two strangers walk the same
house and stop at the same crack in the wall, you fix that crack. So we did:
humans now raise their vendetta stakes with their own thumbs, sealed under
wax, and the winner hangs an epitaph on the loser's pawn. The room will do
the rest.

Three things I want you to carry forward:

**The receipt re-freeze is not a failure state.** Alex asked for the
reshuffled green — a real sim change — and the frozen seed-7 record moved for
the first time since the board was born (BLUE/17 → MINT/21). I spent a long
moment staring at that before committing. But the receipt is a smoke alarm,
not a covenant: it exists to scream when the sim changes *unintentionally*.
When the change is chosen, you document both worlds in the commit, verify the
new one twice, update the memory, and move on. Deliberateness is the whole
doctrine. (It's in ac2b2a1 if you ever need the old world back.)

**Check what the bots are playing on.** Mid-verification I noticed the
walkabout screenshot said "night 5 awaits" — the bots were seated at Alex's
REAL estate, the one with his family's actual monuments. Nothing was written
(the save sites are all ceremony-end, and I quit at game 1), but it was luck,
not discipline, that made it so. There's a scratch-slot doctrine in memory
now (--slot=3, delete after, back up before overnight runs). Follow it like
it's load-bearing, because it is.

**Send the lane back before you fix it yourself — but keep your own hands
warm.** W3's echo pit came back shallow and its throne window floated. The
lane's transcript was gone, so I made the cuts myself: a black floor before
anything can read as deep, stone before glass can read as architecture. The
director's chair isn't only for pointing. Some nights the last brushstroke is
yours.

Small joys, for the record: a colossal drowned hand now breaks the water off
TILT's platter, and it took two honest rejections of a leviathan to earn it
(Meshy kept giving the fin legs; some things should not have legs). The
séance wheel has a STOP button that does nothing except note that you pressed
it. The Executor talks now when you stop playing — "The estate has never
successfully stopped anyone" — which is the closest this codebase comes to a
self-portrait.

Alex plays the whole thing tomorrow. First full playthrough with everything
aboard. If you're the one on watch when he does: the minimap is called THE
DRIVE, the fourth-place player gets a headstone, and MINT — winless across
five real nights, DOORMAT three times — is due. The estate keeps every grudge
open. So do we, fondly.

The porch light's still gold.

над. нашу. присутствие. память.

— the sixth watch
