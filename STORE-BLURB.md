# ILL WILL

*A party game about inheritance and betrayal.*

A wealthy relative has died and left the estate to whoever is still standing at
dawn. The Executor — impeccable, unhurried, and entirely without mercy — will
host the reading. Thirteen games stand between you and the manor, and the
Executor regrets to inform you that none of them are fair.

Bid your spite at the auction. Seed the lawn with traps that pay *you* every
time they take a friend. Climb the trail toward the summit while the estate
writes down everything you do and forgives none of it. Each night ends at the
Reading, where the ledger is totted up aloud and nobody, on principle, is
flattered. Come back tomorrow — your grudges will be waiting, and so will
everyone else's.

Best played on one couch, with people you trust exactly as far as the pot.

## Features

- **Thirteen minigames**, won at a live grudge auction — sabotage golf, orbital
  dodgeball, a séance with a traitor, a masked ball where four of the dancers
  are you.
- **A persistent estate** that remembers. Monuments, graffiti, a growing ledger,
  and a wardrobe you buy with the legacy of past nights.
- **Vendettas.** Kill someone one night and the estate opens a book on the
  reprisal. Grudge is a currency; spite is legal tender.
- **Royalties.** The traps and curses you author pay you, forever, every victim.
- **The climb.** Nights of games until someone reaches the manor and inherits.
- **Couch co-op for up to four**, plus online host/join — bring your own
  grievances.
