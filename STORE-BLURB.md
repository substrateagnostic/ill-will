# ILL WILL

*A party game about inheritance and betrayal.*

A wealthy relative has died and left the estate to whoever is still standing at
dawn. The Executor — impeccable, unhurried, and entirely without mercy — will
host the reading. Fifteen games stand between you and the manor, and the
Executor regrets to inform you that none of them are fair.

Putt your pawn down THE PROCESSION, the estate's funerary board — golf physics
are the dice, deeds are the property, and every grave you pass is holding a
grudge of its own. Bid your spite at the auction. Seed the lawn with traps that
pay *you* every time they take a friend. The estate writes down everything you
do and forgives none of it; each night ends with the Executor delivering a
eulogy composed from your actual failures. Come back tomorrow — your grudges
will be waiting, and so will everyone else's.

Best played on one couch, with people you trust exactly as far as the pot.

## Features

- **THE PROCESSION** — a full board night. Putt to move, buy deeds, survive the
  séance wheel, and watch the Executor reveal every landing like the broadcast
  it is. First to the required deeds is crowned heir; a monument goes up on the
  lawn whether anyone wanted one or not.
- **Fifteen minigames** — sabotage golf, orbital dodgeball, a séance with a
  traitor, a masked ball where four of the dancers are you, and PALLBEARERS,
  a 2v2 coffin-carry where dropping the deceased is noted for the record.
- **A host with a body.** The Executor attends in person — leans in when the
  stakes deserve it, and files a closing eulogy sourced from the night's
  ledger. He has never once raised his voice.
- **Grounds that gossip.** Crows on the fence discuss your past performances,
  verbatim, and fall silent when you walk over. The groundskeeper has opinions
  about his leaves.
- **A persistent estate** that remembers. Monuments, graffiti, a growing
  ledger, a newsreel of the night's disgraces, and a wardrobe you buy with the
  legacy of past nights.
- **Vendettas.** Kill someone one night and the estate opens a book on the
  reprisal. Grudge is a currency; spite is legal tender.
- **Royalties.** The traps and curses you author pay you, forever, every victim.
- **No dead seats.** The eliminated return as ghosts with exactly one small,
  attributed act of mischief. The living are haunted, never robbed.
- **Couch co-op for up to four**, plus online host/join — bring your own
  grievances.
