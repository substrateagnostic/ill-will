# UN Party Game — Ideation (2026-07-03)

Brief: a unique twist on the friendship-ruining party game lineage (Mario Party,
Pummel Party, Super Battle Golf). Success criteria: "wow", "so clever",
"goddamn it", and a core loop that's fun in itself. Excellent graphics, free
assets, buildable by an AI director/builder with visual verification.

## The 20

1. **PAR FOR THE CURSE** (working title) — Sabotage mini-golf where players
   *build the course's traps themselves*. Each hole, everyone drafts a trap
   piece and places it, then everyone must putt through the nightmare they
   collectively authored. Ultimate Chicken Horse × golf. Twist on the twist:
   the SAME hole accretes traps across the whole match, and balls that die
   become permanent gravestones/obstacles — the course becomes a museum of
   everyone's spite.

2. **THE FLOOR IS LAVA (AND YOU ARE THE LAVA)** — Rotating asymmetric: three
   players platform upward, one player IS the rising lava, with surge/spit/
   harden abilities. Score = survival time; everyone takes a turn as the lava.

3. **GREED INC.** — All minigame winnings go into one communal pot displayed
   center-screen. At any moment, any player can GRAB the pot and physically
   run for the exit while everyone hunts them. Banking as betrayal.

4. **ECHO CHAMBER** — Arena combat where every previous round replays as
   ghosts: round 5 contains everyone's past four selves, fighting on. Your
   past mistakes literally kill you. Bounties: your echoes earn you points
   when they land kills in the present.

5. **MARIONETTE PARTY** — Control-swap chaos: you control your own arms but
   someone else's legs. The mapping rotates every 30 seconds. Physics
   slapstick where the betrayal is baked into the controls.

6. **HOUSE RULES** — Between rounds, players secretly vote on rule-mutation
   cards ("gravity halves", "blue player is magnetic", "winner starts on
   fire"). The ruleset compounds into an unrecognizable monster by game end.
   Democracy as a weapon. (Strong as a LAYER on another game.)

7. **CONVEYOR CHAOS KITCHEN** — Competitive sushi chefs sharing one conveyor;
   sabotage each other's orders, steal ingredients, over-season rivals' dishes.

8. **TILT** — The arena is one giant tilting platter balanced on a pin; every
   player's weight tilts it in real physics. Coins make you heavier: more
   influence, worse footing. Last one aboard wins. One mechanic, deep chaos.

9. **CURSE AUCTION** — Between minigames, a silent-bid auction sells targeted
   curses and blessings. The economy of spite: every coin you win becomes
   ammunition against a *specific* friend.

10. **DEAD WEIGHT** — Eliminated players become poltergeists who nudge physics
    objects to torment the living. Solves the genre's cardinal sin (dead =
    bored) by making the dead the most dangerous people in the room.

11. **THE THRONE** — One player holds a throne with god powers (spawn hazards,
    tax coins) and a visible win-timer; the rest must ally to dethrone them —
    then instantly betray each other for the empty seat. Musical-chairs tyranny.

12. **SWAP MEET** — A racer/platformer where all attacks SWAP positions rather
    than deal damage. Every projectile is a potential lead change; first place
    is a curse you wear.

13. **LAST WILL** — Elimination gauntlet where dying players bequeath their
    items/points to a chosen survivor. Strategic dying; kingmaking as gameplay.

14. **WORD FIGHT** — Typed words become physical projectiles sized by rarity;
    "cat" is a pebble, "sesquipedalian" is a wrecking ball. Brains as brawn.

15. **CHAMELEON PROTOCOL** — Physics-brawler with a hidden traitor holding a
    secret objective; deduce and dogpile. Among Us × Gang Beasts.

16. **GRAND HOTEL HAVOC** — Bellhops race deliveries through a shared hotel
    where elevators, doors, and carts are contested resources you can lock,
    steal, and reroute.

17. **ORBITAL DODGEBALL** — Dodgeball on clustered tiny planets with real
    gravity wells. Thrown balls don't despawn — they orbit, and the sky
    slowly fills with everyone's old throws coming back around.

18. **SÉANCE SPORTS** — Every classic minigame (soccer, sumo, race) but half
    the players are ghosts possessing the equipment. The ball has opinions.

19. **THE LONG CON** — Everyone draws a secret win condition ("finish exactly
    2nd", "make green lose", "end broke"). The scoreboard is a decoy; the real
    game is reading what your friends are *actually* trying to do.

20. **TAX SEASON** — Winners pay progressive tax into pots losers can claim;
    hide winnings offshore at audit risk. Rubber-banding as fiscal policy.
    (For the attorney in the room.)

## Shortlist instinct (pre-research)

Strongest on buildability × uniqueness × "goddamn it" engine:
**1 (Par for the Curse), 4 (Echo Chamber), 8 (Tilt), 17 (Orbital Dodgeball),
10 (Dead Weight)** — with 6 (House Rules) and 12 (Swap Meet) as fold-in layers
rather than standalones.

Key insight: #1 is the only one that's *hotseat-native* (turn-based building +
simultaneous putting), meaning it works with one mouse on one couch — zero
hardware friction — while still supporting the Super Battle Golf-style
all-balls-live chaos mode. The accretion twist (course remembers every trap
and every death, all match long) is the "so clever" nobody has shipped.
