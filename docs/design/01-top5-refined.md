# Top 5 — Refined Against Research (2026-07-03)

Research inputs: genre deconstruction (15 atomic betrayal patterns), Super
Battle Golf breakdown (1M+ sales, 4.5-month build; praised: twist +
accessibility; hated: undercooked putt feel, zero comeback mechanics, thin
content), 2023-26 indie hit analysis, market gaps, effort-to-fun ranking.

**The synthesized rule from research:** one verb, excellent feel, variety via
combinatorial drafting + player-authored content (never hand-authored minigame
piles), targeted screwage, *transparent* loser-gets-buff catch-up (the ROUNDS
lesson Mario Party and SBG both fumbled), always-in-play, built-in spectacle.

---

## 1. PAR FOR THE CURSE — sabotage golf on a course that remembers

**One line:** Ultimate Chicken Horse × golf: players draft and place the traps
themselves, then everyone putts *simultaneously* through the nightmare they
co-authored — and the same hole accretes traps and dead balls (gravestones)
across the whole match until it's a haunted Rube Goldberg museum of the
evening's spite.

**Loop per round (~90s):** DRAFT (pick 1 of 3 trap cards; last place drafts
from the visibly nastier CURSED deck — transparent catch-up) → BUILD (place
your trap anywhere; you must survive it too) → PUTT (all balls live at once,
ball-to-ball collisions on; first in the cup wins the round).

**Scoring with teeth:** points for finishing, but *royalties* when YOUR trap
kills someone — cruelty pays, but you have to putt through your own cruelty
(UCH's self-incrimination calculus). Killcam replay with author credit:
"DEATH BY: ALEX'S WINDMILL."

**Accretion (the unshipped idea):** the hole persists. Round 6 is played
through everything placed in rounds 1-5 plus the gravestones of every ball
that died there. The course becomes the match's memory. Players point at the
screen: *that gravestone is from YOUR windmill, you bastard.*

**Absorbed layers:** dead players spend "grudge" for one poltergeist nudge
per putt phase (Dead Weight lite — no one is ever out); optional House Rules
vote every 3 holes.

**Why it wins on the research rubric:** familiar verb + twist (SBG's proven
path) but differentiated on architecture-of-spite, not weapons; fills SBG's
documented gaps (earned catch-up, tight minimal putt feel is achievable when
the verb is drag-aim-release); content = trap pieces (combinatorial, cheap to
extend); hotseat-native — playable with ONE mouse passed around OR
simultaneous with mixed inputs. Zero hardware friction on a couch.

## 2. ECHO CHAMBER — your past selves fight in the present

Arena rounds where every prior round replays as ghosts; by round 5 the arena
teems with everyone's recorded past. Refinements: echoes earn their author
bounty points for present-tense kills (strategic dying: die somewhere *useful*
to future-you); shrinking arena keeps density high; loser drafts the better
weapon. Wow factor: highest of the five. Risks: needs 4 gamepads for couch;
"clever" may outrun "fun" (deterministic ghosts can read as noise, and tuning
that is the whole game).

## 3. TILT — everyone's weight is everyone's problem

One giant platter balanced on a pin; real physics; every step tilts the world
for all. Coins = weight = influence = worse footing. Refinements: grab/throw,
platter cracks in sudden death, between-round power drafts (loser picks
first). Strength: purest one-mechanic depth. Risk: adjacent to the saturated
physics-brawler lane, and the *entire* game is physics-feel tuning — the
hardest thing to get right.

## 4. ORBITAL DODGEBALL — the sky remembers every throw

Dodgeball across tiny planets with real gravity wells; thrown balls never
despawn, they *orbit* — the match slowly fills the sky with everyone's old
throws coming back around (accretion again). Catch = steal + shield for skill
ceiling. Strength: gorgeous, mathematically cheap wow. Risk: orbital aiming
frustration; more "neat" than "betrayal" — weakest goddamn-it engine of the
five.

## 5. DEAD WEIGHT — the dead are the most dangerous people in the room

Elimination brawl where the dead become poltergeists nudging physics objects
to torment the living. Solves the genre's cardinal sin (dead = bored). Risk:
needs a good base brawl *plus* the ghost layer; ghost influence is hard to
balance (too weak = boring dead, too strong = nobody wants to live).

---

## Recommendation

**Build #1, PAR FOR THE CURSE.** It is the only candidate that is
simultaneously: (a) a proven-fun core verb with one clean unshipped twist,
(b) hotseat-native with zero hardware requirements, (c) content-scalable
through a trap library rather than authored minigames, (d) a direct answer to
the #1 and #2 documented complaints about the genre's current 1M-seller, and
(e) structurally able to absorb the best of #2/#5 (accretion IS echo; grudge
nudges ARE dead weight). It converts every research finding into a mechanic.

Fallback if putting feels flat in week one: pivot the same trap-draft/build/
accrete skeleton onto TILT's platter (the skeleton is engine-agnostic).
