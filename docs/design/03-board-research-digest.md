# Board Meta-Layer Research Digest (2026-07-04)

Condensed from deep research (full report in session; key findings durable here).

## Loved vs. resented (evidence-backed)

LOVED: individual autonomy (MP9 "car" era = most hated decision in genre
history); transparency (Jamboree Pro Rules = Nintendo's explicit fix for 25
years of luck resentment: announced win conditions, revealed stars, no
Chance Time); player-authored betrayal (Boo Bell, Pummel items); shops with
real tradeoffs; MP5-7 persistent player-seeded trap spaces (loved, then
abandoned by the series); legible catch-up (TFT loser-first carousel).

RESENTED: hidden end-game bonus stars (#1 grievance); opaque comeback RNG;
dice as the only input (no MP-like uses auctions — open space); late-game
currency inflation (Pummel); downtime watching others' turns (unsolved).

## Prior art verdict on OUR idea

"Every ingredient exists; the synthesis does not." Ultimate Chicken Horse
(96% positive) proves within-match build accretion; **Death Race (1976!)**
had kill-gravestones accumulating until the field clogged — dormant for 50
years; Legacy board games prove permanent-change-as-memory is beloved on
tabletop, never brought to party video games; MP5-7 Orbs prove seeded
persistent traps work on boards. Competitive party arena where traps +
death markers + structures persist as the match's memory: UNOCCUPIED.
Also rhymes with the Nemesis system (grudge memory, famously beloved).

## Five gaps (opportunities)

1. Legible deviousness — persistent seeded traps + full transparency.
   Accretion is inherently transparent: the grudge history is visible ON
   the board.
2. Downtime — between-round phases where EVERYONE acts simultaneously
   (place/shop/bet at once) are underexplored in shipped party games.
3. Turn-order/choice AUCTIONS — zero competition in the genre.
4. Session-to-session accretion — nothing carries scars across game
   nights; Legacy-style campaign memory is unshipped in party video games.
5. Losers as economic actors — death-funded revenge economy (our grudge)
   has no formal implementation anywhere; TFT/Clocktower only gesture.

## Hub-as-menu best practices

Diegetic menu object the group huddles around; physics toys beside the
start button; verbs to mess with friends; economy/cosmetics routed through
the hub with rotating stock; returning-after-loss always yields something
(Hades); small and dense beats large and empty; player marks/graffiti
(Splatoon plaza) = "our room" not "a lobby"; frame hub as recovery.
Failure modes: commute tax, loading-as-traversal, big-empty, hub worse
than a menu. (Note: SBG "den hub" could not be verified — unconfirmed.)
